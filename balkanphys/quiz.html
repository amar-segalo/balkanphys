Quiz and Eligibility

Students can register in teams of 3-5 competitors. Any medical student who has passed an examination in physiology is eligible to participate. The quiz will take place on Friday, April 9 and will be conducted in English. We have gone to lengths to ensure BPC is a fair competition. Dr. Michael Chirillo from the University of Texas at Austin is our fun and impartial quiz master. With the help of Dr. Amina Valjevac from the University of Sarajevo, he has made sure that the quiz will be engaging, challenging, and impartial. Dr. Chirillo will be the only person to have seen the actual quiz questions. You can read more about the people behind the quiz here.

Thursday morning, each student will take a preliminary multiple-choice quiz covering general medical physiology. In the afternoon, the teams with the top average exam scores will go head-to-head for some friendly competition in multiple rounds of oral quizzes. Awards will be given to the top three teams during a closing ceremony before everyone goes out to celebrate on the town. Saturday we’ve planned a bunch of optional excursions (including rafting!) so you have more time to spend with your fellow competitors and faculty members.

Quiz format

Multiple-choice test

Our quiz day begins with an individual written exam lasting 1 hour that will consist of roughly 70 multiple-choice questions. The competitors will be asked to select only one correct answer from the answer choices. Each competitor will receive the same quiz. The team's average score will be calculated and the top-scoring teams will move on to the oral quizzes.

Oral quizzes

The oral quizzes will be played as a seed-style tournament over multiple rounds. Quarterfinal, semifinal, and final rounds will be played as team-on-team oral quizzes. Our quiz Master will guide the teams through the rules and through a variety of surprise rounds. So expect interesting categories, real-life patient cases, mini-games, physiology trivia, and of course a lot of fun! Check out study resources for the types of questions you can expect to be asked during the quizzes.

Awards ceremony

Awards will be given to the top 3 teams and certificates of appreciation will be given to all competitors and to their faculty mentors. During the day, you will have ample time to meet people during oral quizzes, chat, and have a great time!

Example multiple-choice questions

1. Pressure in the intrapleural space is normally ___________ pressure inside the alveoli at the beginning of inspiration.

  • greater than
  • less than
  • equal to

2. Pressure in the intrapleural space is normally ___________ pressure inside the alveoli during the end inspiration.

  • greater than
  • less than
  • equal to

3. Massive output from the sympathetic nervous system will result in all of the following except:

  • Release of adrenaline (also known as epinephrine) into the bloodstream
  • Vasoconstriction of arterioles leading to skeletal muscle
  • An increase in heart rate
  • A general increase in catabolism
  • Pupillary dilation (mydriasis)

Example clinical case and questions

Amina has had heart problems for years and doesn’t always follow her physician’s suggestions. She continues to eat poorly and not take her medicine. At night, she begins noticing she’s having a hard time sleeping flat on her back. She feels like she can’t breathe well. She is able to sleep by putting pillows under her back and finally finds out that it’s much easier to breathe when she sleeps sitting up in a chair. Her breathing becomes more and more difficult over several weeks and finally Amina decides to go to the emergency department. On arrival, she is clearly in distress and is having a hard time breathing. Her heart rate is 124 and her blood pressure is 176/104. You notice her legs are swollen and when you press your thumb into her shin, the imprint stays in her skin. A chest x-ray shows a very large heart and fluid in her lungs. She is diagnosed with acute heart failure and the emergency doctors begin to make a treatment plan for Amina.

  1. Explain the most likely physiological mechanism that explains why Amina’s legs are swollen when she arrives at the emergency department?
  2. What forces play a role in filtration and absorption of fluid in capillaries?
  3. Compared to a person with a normally functioning heart, fluid pressure in Amina’s veins is higher, lowever, or the same?
  4. Why might you think it’s easier for Amina to sleep sitting up? What is the physiological explanation for this?
  5. Ultimately, the doctors decide to give Amina furosemide, a drug which acts as a diuretic and helps get rid of the excess fluid in her body. Furosemide works by blocking transporters in the thick ascending limb of the nephron. Using your knowledge of basic physiology, explain how this mechanism helps Amina excrete excess fluid.
